Red Enchiladas Recipe

Description

This mouthwatering enchilada recipe takes you on a culinary journey through the vibrant flavors of Mexico. Whether you're a fan of Mexican cuisine or looking to explore new culinary horizons, these enchiladas are sure to impress your taste buds and leave you craving more. Enjoy them as a satisfying meal for any occasion!

Ingredients:

  • 4 guajillo chiles, opened, cleaned, and seedless.
  • 4 ancho chiles, opened, cleaned, and seedless.
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced.
  • ¼ teaspoon of Mexican dried oregano.
  • Salt and pepper to taste.
  • 12 corn tortillas.
  • 2 cups of shredded pork or chicken (optional).
  • 1 ½ cups of crumbled fresh cheese.
  • ½ cup of finely chopped white onion.
  • ⅓ cup of vegetable oil.

Steps:

  1. Let's start with the sauce: Lightly toast the chiles on a hot griddle, pressing them with a spatula, making sure not to burn them. (This step only takes a few seconds on each side).
  2. Once the chiles are toasted, place them in a saucepan with water and simmer for 15 minutes, or until they are soft.
  3. Remove the saucepan from heat and let the chiles cool for 10 to 15 minutes. (Remember, they should be soft and tender).
  4. After the chiles have cooled, drain them and place them in a blender along with the garlic cloves; add ½ cup of clean water and blend until you obtain a smooth sauce. (If the sauce is too thick or the chiles weren't ground properly, it's necessary to strain it through a fine sieve). Season with oregano, salt, and pepper.
  5. Preheat the oven to 350℉ (180℃) to keep the enchiladas warm while you finish assembling them.
  6. In a large skillet, add 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil and heat over medium heat. (Add the oil gradually, as needed, while frying the tortillas, as adding it all at once will make the tortillas absorb it and break).
  7. Dip each tortilla into the sauce, making sure both sides are well moistened.
  8. Then, place the tortilla in the hot oil and fry on both sides. (This only takes a few seconds). Place the enchilada on a plate. Add more oil to the skillet as needed and continue the process of frying the tortillas: dip them in the sauce first, then in the oil. Place the enchiladas on the preheated oven to keep them warm while you finish frying the remaining tortillas.
  9. To serve the enchiladas, first place your choice of filling in the center of the tortilla, then fold or roll it as shown in the picture above.
  10. Sprinkle the enchiladas with cheese and onion; add any other garnish to your liking, and... enjoy!
