Cajeta Empanadas

Description

Ahhh, empanadas! They are those delicious golden crescents on the outside and warm on the inside. If you love the taste of cajeta (dulce de leche) and the crispy texture of empanadas, don't miss trying these cajeta empanadas at home.

Empanadas are usually round dough discs with different fillings, folded to give them a crescent shape, and the filling can be sweet or savory. Its name comes from the word "empanar," which means to cover something with dough.

Ingredients:

(For 10 empanadas)

  • Flour (3 cups)
  • Vegetable shortening (250 g)
  • Room temperature Lager beer (1/2 can)
  • Refined sugar (4 tablespoons)
  • Baking powder (1/2 teaspoon)
  • Refined sugar for coating (1/2 cup)
  • Cinnamon for coating (1/2 tablespoon)
  • Burnt cajeta (dulce de leche) 1 kg

Steps:

  1. Start by combining the dry ingredients: refined sugar and baking powder with the vegetable shortening. Mix well to incorporate the ingredients.
  2. Gradually add the half can of beer and knead well as you add the liquid. Mix well until you obtain a homogeneous dough that doesn't stick to your hands.
  3. Form medium-sized balls and let them rest at room temperature for 15 minutes. Then flatten them with your hands or a tortilla press to make them more uniform.
  4. In the center of each "tortilla," place two or three tablespoons of cajeta. Fold them well and press the edges to prevent the filling from leaking. Preferably, fold them inward to make them more compact. Repeat with all the dough balls.
  5. Arrange the empanadas on a baking sheet lined with wax paper, leaving a slight space between them to allow them to breathe.
  6. Bake at 180℃ (350℉) for 35 minutes. Remove the tray from the oven as soon as they are golden brown and let them rest for five minutes.
  7. Mix the refined sugar with the cinnamon and sprinkle it over the empanadas. We recommend doing this as soon as they come out of the oven; it will give them an irresistible touch.
